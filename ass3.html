2024 BMW M3 Expert Review

BMW has something special in store for the fourth model year of the G80 M3. Remember the outstanding M5 CS? The engineers in Bavaria are now applying that treatment to BMW's compact sport sedan to offer a four-door, slightly less focused sibling to the mighty M4 CSL. Once we get some time behind the wheel, we'll be able to determine how it compares to the Audi RS5 Competition, although the CS is in a class of its own as a compact high-performance four-door for around $120,000.

Technology

Along with the standard 3 Series, the M3 received a serious tech upgrade for 2023. Starting last year, the M3 now showcases a 12.3-inch instrument cluster display alongside a 14.9-inch touchscreen infotainment system, and both screens are housed under the same piece of curved glass. Apple CarPlay, Android Auto, and integrated navigation are all standard, plus the infotainment features BMW's cool though gimmicky gesture controls.

Comparison Test high-performance

We've had the privilege of driving the current M3 back-to-back with a pair of the best driving sport sedans in the industry. In a comparison test against the Alfa Romeo Giulia Quadrifoglio, we favored the Alfa's more rewarding driving experience and overflowing personality over the M3's superb engine and reasonable suspension comfort. We compared the M3 Competition to the Cadillac CT4-V Blackwing in another four-door face-off.
